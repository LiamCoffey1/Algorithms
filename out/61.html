Introducing the New President

Introducing the University of Regina's Eighth President and Vice-Chancellor

Dr. Jeff Keshen

Following a year-long, rigorous, national search, the University of Regina is pleased to announce Dr. Jeff Keshen as the institution’s incoming president and vice-chancellor.

Dr. Jeff Keshen will assume the role of president and vice-chancellor beginning July 1, 2021.

About Dr. Keshen

As an academic, researcher, and administrator, Keshen has held a variety of roles at universities across Canada. He is an award-winning author, nationally-celebrated educator, and accomplished volunteer.

Keshen comes from Memorial University’s Grenfell Campus where he has served as vice-president since 2017. During his time at Memorial, Keshen championed student success, grew research programs, and enhanced the institution’s commitment to equity, diversity, inclusion, and Indigenization.

Dr. Keshen Discusses the U of R

As we look towards our 50th anniversary and beyond, Keshen noted that the U of R’s strong foundation was a key reason for applying to be the next President.

“The U of R is connected to, and is really involved with, its community,” says Keshen. “It’s young and young at heart. It’s open to experimentation. I think it’s a tremendously exciting place with an amazing future.”

Throughout the presidential search process – which engaged a search committee comprised of students, faculty, staff, and alumni – Keshen’s track-record made him the ideal fit for the role.

We are proud to welcome Dr. Jeff Keshen and his family to the University community.
